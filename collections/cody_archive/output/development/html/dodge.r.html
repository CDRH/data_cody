Dodge, Richard Irving, 1827-1895

Richard Irving Dodge (1827-1895), a career soldier, served in the U.S. Army from the age of twenty-one until four years before his death at age sixty-eight. Between 1881 and 1882, Irving acted as aide-de-camp to General William Tecumseh Sherman; in 1882 he received his promotion to colonel and continued on duty in the West.
