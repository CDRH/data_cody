      <h3>SERMONS IN RHYME:
ON EVERYDAY TEXTS.</h3>

      <h3>IV.—THE RED INDIAN IN LEEDS.</h3>

      <p>"<span class="persName">Short Bull</span>" and "<span class="persName">Kicking Bear</span>" are amongst the Sioux Indians of Buffalo Bill's Wild West Show now exhibiting in Leeds.—<em class="hi">Daily Paper.</em>
      </p>

      <div class="inline_figure">
         <div class="p">[illustration]</div>
         <span class="ab credit">E A H</span>
      </div>
      <span class="lg">
         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l">SHORT BULL and Kicking Bear,</span>
            <span class="l">Indians Red, and Indians rare,</span>
            <span class="l indent">Let me put some questions;</span>
            <span class="l">Then, perhaps, though Red you be,</span>
            <span class="l">You will say in language free,</span>
            <span class="l">What you think of what you see,</span>
            <span class="l indent">And may make suggestions.</span>
         </span>

         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l">This I said, and this I wrote,</span>
            <span class="l">Putting in, you'll please to note,</span>
            <span class="l indent">Many little posers;</span>
            <span class="l">How brave Kicking Bear replied,</span>
            <span class="l">How Short Bull did then decide,</span>
            <span class="l">Where they erred, or where they lied,</span>
            <span class="l indent">I'll not tell you, no, sirs.</span>
         </span>

         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l">"What d'ye think of Mayor Cooke?<a href="../doc/n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
            </span>
            <span class="l">Tell me, Kicking Bear, and look,</span>
            <span class="l indent">You make no digression."</span>
            <span class="l">"Mayor Cooke," the Sioux essayed, </span>
            <span class="l">"Me much makee great afraid,</span>
            <span class="l">By his side me much in shade,</span>
            <span class="l indent">That is true confession."</span>
         </span>

         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l">"Now, Short Bull, your turn comes next, </span>
            <span class="l">Don't evade on false pretext.</span>
            <span class="l indent">What think you of Spark, now?"</span>
            <span class="l">"Doctor Sparkee,<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a> makee noise,</span>
            <span class="l">Please the little girls and boys,</span>
            <span class="l">In Far West he'd givee joys,</span>
            <span class="l indent">But you keep it dark now."</span>
         </span>

         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l">"Kicking Bear," I did proceed,</span>
            <span class="l">"How would you our Archie read,</span>
            <span class="l indent">Our great water drinker?"</span>
            <span class="l">"Archie figee me like not,</span>
            <span class="l">Though drink water is most hot,</span>
            <span class="l">And he talkee muchee rot,</span>
            <span class="l indent">I of that am thinker."</span>
         </span>

         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l">"Once again, then, Short Bull, say,</span>
            <span class="l">What think you of our Sir J.—</span>
            <span class="l indent">That is, Sir James Kitson?"<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a>
            </span>
            <span class="l">"Man of iron, very strong, </span>
            <span class="l">Fightee bravely, right or wrong,</span>
            <span class="l">Bigger be before 'tis long, </span>
            <span class="l indent">For M. P. a fit son."</span>
         </span>

         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l">"Kicking Bear, you've seen the Grand,</span>
            <span class="l">Nicest theatre in the land,—</span>
            <span class="l indent">What's your own opinion?"</span>
            <span class="l">"Much pretty, music sweet,</span>
            <span class="l">Likee dancing of the feet,</span>
            <span class="l">Tomahawk would make complete</span>
            <span class="l indent">Manager's dominion."</span>
         </span>

         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l">"Roundhay Park<a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a> you've seen, Short Bull, </span>
            <span class="l">When with Sunday people full,</span>
            <span class="l indent">Is it not most charming?"</span>
            <span class="l">"If horse ridee very well,</span>
            <span class="l">But too far the good to tell,</span>
            <span class="l">Bring it nearer to your swell,</span>
            <span class="l indent">Or let out for farming."</span>
         </span>

         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l">Then, before I spoke again,</span>
            <span class="l">They went at it might and main,</span>
            <span class="l indent">Many things denouncing;</span>
            <span class="l">Briggate and its fine arcade,</span>
            <span class="l">The new Post, the Boar Lane shade,</span> 
            <span class="l">Kirkstall Abbey, Leeds's trade, </span>
            <span class="l indent">All came in for trouncing.</span>
         </span>
      </span>


   </div>



   <div class="note">Note 1: Alf Cooke (1843-1902), of Alf Cooke printworks,  was Mayor of Leeds, England, in 1890.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: Dr. William Spark (1823-1897), composer and Borough Organist of Leeds.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: Sir James Kitson (1835-1911), 1st Baron Airedale, was a British politician, a Member of Parliament, and a prominent Unitarian in Leeds.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: Roundhay Park, Leeds, West Yorkshire, where the first public trolley system (electric tram with overhead power) was installed in Britain in 1891.</div>
