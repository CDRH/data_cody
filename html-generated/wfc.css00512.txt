   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Ottumwa, Iowa, on October 2, 1896. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: Cody, Wyoming.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: "Buffalo men" are the investors in Shoshone Irrigation Company, including <span class="persName">Rumsey</span>, Bleistein, and <span class="persName">Gerrans</span>.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: "Stokes" is possibly Edward S. Stokes (1841-1901), at one time a railroad and oil magnate and businessman. Stokes was an owner of Hoffman House, an elegant hotel in Manhattan where Cody was often a guest. Cody may have offered stock to Stokes to invest in the Cody Canal.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 5: "Red Lodge [Montana] firms" are the suppliers for the irrigation project to whom Shoshone Irrigation Company owed money.</div>
