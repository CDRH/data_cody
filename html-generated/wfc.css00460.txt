      <span class="salute">Dear <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://codyarchive.org/doc/beck.g">George</a>
         </span>
      </span>



      <p>
         <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://codyarchive.org/doc/alger.h">Alger</a>
         </span> 
         <del>think</del> writes me you have ordered winter groceries &amp;c— If so countermand the order And let no more contracts for winter work. its all too high. 7c a yard for loose dirt is all out of reason. I will put a grader on in the Spring &amp; <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="move​ &#xA;    ">moove</a> all loose dirt for 4c  &amp; I think for 3c. George I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="haven't​ &#xA;    ">havent</a> the money to go on this winter &amp; that Carey Act is so uncertain. <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://codyarchive.org/doc/salsbury.n">Salsbury</a>
         </span> writes me no business man will now touch it. Now old Pard I will do all I can to keep you <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="Solid​ &#xA;    ">Sollid</a>. But you must let me down just as easy &amp; quietly as possible. You can tell them its to expensive to try to work in winter— that work will be resumed in the Spring. <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://codyarchive.org/doc/vandreveldt.e">Van Dreveldt</a>
         </span> ought to be through— So he can be let off. Is <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://codyarchive.org/doc/heckart.t">Heckert</a>
         </span> going to stay there. I would certainly not pay him if he does not stay &amp; work. If you think best We can keep my teams at work all winter— Heck said he could do that whole job with our own outfit in 60 days— Your last estimate <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="don't​ &#xA;    ">dont</a> look like it. Now George. You know the situation and act quick. So as to save our credit—</p>

      <p>I am blue all over—</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <br/>Bill </span>
