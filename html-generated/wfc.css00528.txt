      <div class="note">Buffalo Bill's Wild West</div>

      <span class="dateline">Burlington<a href="#n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
         <br/>

Aug 17<a href="#n02" class="internal_link">2</a>
      </span>

      <span class="salute">My Dear <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/beck.g">Beck</a>
         </span>
      </span>

      <p>I find that Jones<a href="#n03" class="internal_link">3</a> is very unpopular in Kewanee<a href="#n04" class="internal_link">4</a> and I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="don't​ &#xA;    ">dont</a> think he will ever go there again. And <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="it's​ &#xA;    ">its</a> Most Necessary that we should have a salesman there Mr. E. D. Mayhew Post Master<a href="#n05" class="internal_link">5</a> wants the appointment for Kewanee and the county Henry Co. I wish you would send him the appointment at <u>once</u> As He wants to send some people out in Sep. And he wants to go to Chicago to see Eustis<a href="#n06" class="internal_link">6</a> to make rates. he <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="also​ &#xA;    ">allso</a> wants an Exhibit of grain to show at the Fair.<a href="#n07" class="internal_link">7</a> Will you kindly see that Mayhew gets his appointment and at once. Without putting off—</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Very truly yours</span>

         <br/>W. F. Cody</span>

      <p>PS. I have never heard if canal was repaired.</p>

   <div class="bibliography">
      <p>
         <a name="n01.note"/>Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Burlington, Iowa, on August 17, 1898. <a href="#n01.ref">  [back]
            </a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n02.note"/>Note 2: The year is not inscribed by Cody but is 1898.<a href="#n02.ref">  [back]
            </a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n03.note"/>Note 3: "Jones" is likely C. B. Jones, a land agent from Galesburg, Illinois, who brought settlers to the Big Horn Basin. <a href="#n03.ref">  [back]
            </a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n04.note"/>Note 4: Kewanee, a city of Henry County in northwestern Illinois, lies about 45 miles northwest of Peoria, Illinois. The town became a prairie boom town along the railroad tracks in 1854. In 1856 those tracks were appropriated by the Chicago, Burlington &amp; Quincy Railroad. <a href="#n04.ref">  [back]
            </a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n05.note"/>Note 5: "Mr. E. D. Mayhew Post Master" is Ernest Davis Mayhew (1859-1917), a merchant in Kewanee, Henry County, Illinois, who dealt in timber, ice, coal, and grocery merchandise.<a href="#n05.ref">  [back]
            </a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n06.note"/>Note 6: P. S. Eustis was the general passenger agent in Chicago, Illinois, for the Burlington Route of the Northern Pacific Railway that went from Chicago to Yellowstone National Park.<a href="#n06.ref">  [back]
            </a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n07.note"/>Note 7: "Exhibit of grain to show at the Fair" likely refers to samples of grain grown in the Big Horn Basin.<a href="#n07.ref">  [back]
            </a>
      </p>
