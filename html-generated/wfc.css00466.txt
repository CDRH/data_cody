      <span class="salute">My Dear <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/beck.g">George</a>
         </span>,</span>

      <p>I wish you would write me c/o Annex <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="Auditorium​ &#xA;    ">Audtorium</a>. Hotel Chicago And tell me how the work is progressing. what time you expect to finish to Sulphur Creek.<a name="n02.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n02.note" class="ref">2</a> and if you have money enough. what time you can finish so as to put water on the 25000 acres. have you tried the graders? hope you have <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="moved​ &#xA;    ">mooved</a> away from Marquette. Is <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/rumsey.b">Rumsey</a>
         </span> still there. has he paid in the $5000 for his Brother<a name="n03.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n03.note" class="ref">3</a>? Hope you and Rumsey took up the Townsite<a name="n04.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n04.note" class="ref">4</a> what are you doing about Townsite Say George. <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/gourard.f">Frank Gourard</a>
         </span>
         <a name="n05.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n05.note" class="ref">5</a> Said he was to bring fifteen teams of horses from your ranch for him. Also that I could have some hogs from your place. If so send Frank an order for them to Sheridan. These questions I have asked of you will be asked me when I get East. So please answer at once.</p>

      <h6 class="fw">2</h6>

      <p>I have to day recd wires from Mondell<a name="n06.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n06.note" class="ref">6</a> and <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/burke.j">Burke</a>
         </span>
         <a name="n07.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n07.note" class="ref">7</a> that President<a name="n08.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n08.note" class="ref">8</a> will sign contracts Monday 23d. I go to Cheyenne tomorrow to consult with State Land board<a name="n09.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n09.note" class="ref">9</a> &amp; <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/mead.e">Meade</a>
         </span>. And do all I can to hurry matters there. Get up Water bonds for Nagle<a name="n10.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n10.note" class="ref">10</a>— Recd wire from <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/alger.h">Alger</a>
         </span> that he would ship Samples of grain to Nagle What did you do about Seed for Nagle and our selves? If Nothing. why not write Alger to attend to it. Nagle and party he says will leave Chicago Apr. 15 18<u>th</u> Billings. leave Billings 19. arrive on their lands 25<u>th</u>  Can you have water in Ditch for them? With all my work and worry I have got to go rehearsing and jump into a hard summers work. A tired man— But I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="won't​ &#xA;    ">wont</a> care. If I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="don't​ &#xA;    ">dont</a> have to bear the blunt of bad management on that Ditch. So for Gods sake George— Keep things straight there. </p>

      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Yours </span>

         <br/>Bill
</span>

   <div class="bibliography">
      <p>
         <a name="n01.note"> </a>Note 1: Cody overwrites the imprinted 1895 with a handwritten "6" indicating the year is 1896.<a href="#n01.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n02.note"> </a>Note 2: Sulphur Creek is a natural streambed located west of Cody, Wyoming, flowing north toward the Shoshone River along the base of Cedar Mountain.<a href="#n02.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n03.note"> </a>Note 3: Bronson Rumsey's brother was Laurence Dana Rumsey, Sr., 1849-1917.<a href="#n03.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n04.note"> </a>Note 4: Cody, Wyoming.<a href="#n04.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n05.note"> </a>Note 5: Frank Gourard: Cody misspells the surname of Frank Grouard.<a href="#n05.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n06.note"> </a>Note 6: Mondell is likely Frank Wheeler Mondell (1860-1939) who served in the House of Representatives from 1899 until 1923. He came to Wyoming in 1887 and helped develop the town of Newcastle along with oil and coal interests. Mondell was chairman of the Committee on Irrigation of Arid Lands for the 58th and 59th Congresses.<a href="#n06.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n07.note"> </a>Note 7: "Burke" is likely John M. Burke, though it is possibly Carlton Burke, a foreman working on the Cody Canal.<a href="#n07.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n08.note"> </a>Note 8: "President" is likely a reference to President Grover Cleveland, whom Cody was hoping would soon sign over the lands for settlement in the Big Horn Basin under the Carey Act of 1894.<a href="#n08.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n09.note"> </a>Note 9: The Wyoming government body overseeing the Carey Act was the State Land Board in Cheyenne.<a href="#n09.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n10.note"> </a>Note 10: S. V. Nagle, an associate of the firm of F. A. Nagle Commission Merchants of Chicago, attempted to recruit settlers to the lands in the Big Horn Basin that were to be irrigated by the Cody Canal.<a href="#n10.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
