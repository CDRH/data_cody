   <div class="note">Note 1: Telegram is handwritten by clerk or William F. Cody assignee. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: The year is not inscribed but was 1895.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Waterville, Maine, on July 17, 1895, and in Bangor, Maine, on July 18, 1895.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: McTague Hotel: James Hugh McTague (1858-1926), owner of a catering service in Omaha, Nebraska, who opened a basement saloon with partner Charles Little under the name of Little &amp; McTague. McTague later opened a European-style hotel in Omaha, Nebraska, before relocating to Missouri. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 5: "Creighton" is likely John A. Creighton (1831-1907), pioneer businessman and philanthropist from Omaha, Nebraska, who founded Creighton University, owned a railroad company, and was Vice-President of the First National Bank of Omaha.</div>
