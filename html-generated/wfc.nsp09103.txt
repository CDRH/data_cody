      <h3>The Queen at the Wild West Show.</h3>
      <div class="milestone line"> </div>
      <span class="lg">
         <h4>THE SONG OF PUNCHIWATHA.<a name="n01.ref"> </a>
            <a href="#n01.note" class="ref">[1]</a>
         </h4>
         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l indented">Would you hear how Col. Cody</span>
            <span class="l">Gave his wondrous exhibition,</span>
            <span class="l">Of his Indians on the war path,</span>
            <span class="l">In the sight of <span class="persName">
                  <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/victoria.q">Queen Victoria</a>
               </span>;</span>
            <span class="l">Listen to this simple story</span>
            <span class="l">From the mouth of Punchiwatha;</span>
         </span>

         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l indented">When she reached the exhibition,</span>
            <span class="l">Lo! a box near the arena</span>
            <span class="l">Was propared for her reception;</span>
            <span class="l">Whitely too, and Col. Russell<a name="n02.ref"> </a>
               <a href="#n02.note" class="ref">[2]</a>
            </span>
            <span class="l">And the wily Townsend Percy<a name="n03.ref"> </a>
               <a href="#n03.note" class="ref">[3]</a>
            </span>
            <span class="l">As an escort to the lady,</span>
            <span class="l">To the empress of the North Land.</span>
            <span class="l">Then the Indians and the cowboys,</span>
            <span class="l">And the wonderful vacqueras,</span>
            <span class="l">Raced and charged and whirled before her,</span>
            <span class="l">Stopped the coach and wheeled and circled,</span>
            <span class="l">Like some birds of brilliant plumage</span>
            <span class="l">Round a carcass on the mountains.</span>
            <span class="l">Balls of glass were thrown and shattered</span>
            <span class="l">By the clever Col. Cody,</span>
            <span class="l">Like Wade-no the magician;</span>
            <span class="l">Ladies, too, there wielded rifles</span>
            <span class="l">Even as the strong man Kwa-sind.</span>
         </span>

         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l indented">To the queen came Ogila-sa,</span>
            <span class="l">Sioux chief,<a name="n04.ref"> </a>
               <a href="#n04.note" class="ref">[4]</a> and bowed before her;</span>
            <span class="l">He across the Big Sea-Water</span>
            <span class="l">Came to see the queen and empress,</span>
            <span class="l">And will tell the wondrous story</span>
            <span class="l">Oft-times in the Wild West wigwams, </span>
            <span class="l">In the days of the hereafter.</span>
         </span>

         <span class="lg">
            <span class="l indented">To the queen, too, the papooses,</span>
            <span class="l">Dusky little Indian babies,</span>
            <span class="l">Were presented, and she touched them</span>
            <span class="l">Gently with a royal finger;</span>
            <span class="l">That the squaws, the happy mothers,</span>
            <span class="l">Might go back upon the Kee-way-din.</span>
            <span class="l">On the Home-Wind o'er the water.</span>
            <span class="l">To the land of the Ojibiways.</span>
            <span class="l">To the land of the Dacotahs.</span>
            <span class="l">To the mountains of the prairie.</span>
            <span class="l">Singing gaily all the praises</span>
            <span class="l">Of the gentle queen and empress,</span>
            <span class="l">And the wonders of the North Land.</span>
         </span>

         <span class="byline">
            <em class="hi">—London Punch.</em>
         </span>

      </span>

   <div class="bibliography">
      <p>
         <a name="n01.note"> </a>Note 1: To honor Queen Victoria's private visit to Buffalo Bill's Wild West on May 5, 1887, <em class="hi">Punch, or The London Charivari,</em> a weekly British magazine of humor and satire from 1841 to 1992, created <em class="hi">The Song of Punchiwatha,</em> which closely resembled Longfellow's poem <em class="hi">Hiawatha.</em>
         <a href="#n01.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n02.note"> </a>Note 2: The American Exhibition was represented by Americans Colonel Henry S. Russell (1838-1905), president, and John Robinson Whitley (1843-1922), director-general, who managed to ensure a place for Buffalo Bill's Wild West to perform at Earl's Court.<a href="#n02.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n03.note"> </a>Note 3: Townsend Percy, the official Press Representative for the American Exhibition and a member of the Royal Geographical Society of London, was an author and lecturer.<a href="#n03.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n04.note"> </a>Note 4: Ogila-sa (Ogilesha, Ogle Sa, Ogilasa, and Ógle Lúta among other variants) was <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/red.shirt">Chief Red Shirt</a>
         </span>.<a href="#n04.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
