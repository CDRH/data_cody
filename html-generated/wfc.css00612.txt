      <div class="note">DEPARTMENT OF THE INTERIOR <br/> WASHINGTON </div>
      <span class="dateline">April 6, 1916.</span>
  
      <span class="salute">My dear Mr. Cody:</span>




      <p>Your letter of March 31, is received and the Department notes with gratification that it is your intention to see that the Interior Department is supplied with a set of films of the Pine Ridge Indian Res., in accordance with our several letters and your reply to the same.</p>
      <p>As there have been several inquiries made for the use of these reels, the Department will appreciate an early delivery of the same.</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Cordially yours,</span>
         <br/>Chief Clerk.<a href="../doc/n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
    Mr. W. F. Cody,
    Cody, Wyoming.
</span>
   </div>



  <div class="note">Note 1: Ezekiel Jarman Ayers (1866-1931) was Chief Clerk, Department of Interior, Superintendent of Buildings, and Acting Assistant Secretary of Interior; he was appointed by Secretary of War Franklin K. Lane in 1911, serving until his resignation in 1920.</div>
