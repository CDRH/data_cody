      <span class="dateline">Chicago Jan. 27 / 191__<a name="n01.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n01.note" class="ref">1</a>
      </span>


      <span class="salute">Dear <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://codyarchive.org/doc/tait.j">Tait</a>
         </span>.</span>


      <p>Your letter came Johnny got in with some great dope yesterday is being gotten ready to go to Washington along with the Indian <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="pictures​ &#xA;    ">picturers</a>.<a name="n02.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n02.note" class="ref">2</a> Johnny may go in to old Mexico with a movie outfit.<a name="n03.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n03.note" class="ref">3</a> Dont suppose you want any of that. Eh?</p>

      <p>Ill be back from Washington in a week.</p>
      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Yours</span>

         <br/>Governor
</span>
   </div>


   <div class="bibliography">
      <p>
         <a name="n01.note"> </a>Note 1: Although Cody did not include the year when dating this letter, the context indicates that it was written in 1914. Cody went to Washington, D.C. in February 1914 to show his film <em class="hi">The Indian Wars</em> to audiences that included senior officials in the Interior and War Departments.<a href="#n01.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
