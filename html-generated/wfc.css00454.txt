      <span class="salute">Dear <span class="persName">Beck</span>—</span>


      <p>I have not been able to write a letter to any one. Since I got near N. Y. then came Baltimore &amp; Washington<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a>. And to day we did not get here until 11 am— had to make parade &amp; just finished first show. I recd your letters— I have just wired <span class="persName">Hymer</span> &amp; <span class="persName">Alger</span> to send you word <u>quick</u> to make ditch 20 feet wide on bottom. I have written 25 letters at least saying no use to build a little ditch I came near falling dead. &amp; I some some tall cussing when I read your letter about a 15 foot ditch after all the letters I have

   <div class="note">Note 1: The year is not inscribed by Cody but is 1895 as determined by the Routes List: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Richmond, Virginia, on October 4, 1895.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: Cody implies he is very busy with visitors while Buffalo Bill's Wild West performs near New York, Baltimore, and Washington, D.C.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: "Mr. Foote" is Robert Foote (1834-1916), a democrat and state senator representing Johnson County, Wyoming. Foote led the effort against the Carey Act in Wyoming. Cody threatened to quit the project as a result of Foote's accusation that Cody was subverting the interests of Wyoming and of the "real people" of the Big Horn Basin.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: Michael Hoke Smith (1855-1931) was the 19th United States Secretary of the Interior from March 6, 1893, to September 1, 1896. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 5: Francis Emroy Warren (1844-1929), a Republican Senator representing Wyoming during 1890-1893 before returning to his business pursuits. Warren was re-elected to the Senate from March 4, 1895, to November 24, 1929, but died while in office.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 6: "Him" refers to Wyoming Senator Robert Foote in Foote's opposition to the Cody Canal.</div>
