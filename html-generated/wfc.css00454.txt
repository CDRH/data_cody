      <p>I have not been able to write a letter to any one. Since I got near N. Y. then came Baltimore &amp; Washington<a name="n02.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n02.note" class="ref">2</a>. And to day we did not get here until 11 am— had to make parade &amp; just finished first show. I recd your letters— I have just wired <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://codyarchive.org/doc/hymer.w">Hymer</a>
         </span> &amp; <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://codyarchive.org/doc/alger.h">Alger</a>
         </span> to send you word <u>quick</u> to make ditch 20 feet wide on bottom. I have written 25 letters at least saying no use to build a little ditch I came near falling dead. &amp; I some some tall cussing when I read your letter about a 15 foot ditch after all the letters I have

   <div class="bibliography">
      <p>
         <a name="n01.note"> </a>Note 1: The year is not inscribed by Cody but is 1895 as determined by the Routes List: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Richmond, Virginia, on October 4, 1895.<a href="#n01.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n02.note"> </a>Note 2: Cody implies he is very busy with visitors while Buffalo Bill's Wild West performs near New York, Baltimore, and Washington, D.C.<a href="#n02.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n03.note"> </a>Note 3: "Mr. Foote" is Robert Foote (1834-1916), a democrat and state senator representing Johnson County, Wyoming. Foote led the effort against the Carey Act in Wyoming. Cody threatened to quit the project as a result of Foote's accusation that Cody was subverting the interests of Wyoming and of the "real people" of the Big Horn Basin.<a href="#n03.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n04.note"> </a>Note 4: Michael Hoke Smith (1855-1931) was the 19th United States Secretary of the Interior from March 6, 1893, to September 1, 1896. <a href="#n04.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n05.note"> </a>Note 5: Francis Emroy Warren (1844-1929), a Republican Senator representing Wyoming during 1890-1893 before returning to his business pursuits. Warren was re-elected to the Senate from March 4, 1895, to November 24, 1929, but died while in office.<a href="#n05.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n06.note"> </a>Note 6: "Him" refers to Wyoming Senator Robert Foote in Foote's opposition to the Cody Canal.<a href="#n06.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
