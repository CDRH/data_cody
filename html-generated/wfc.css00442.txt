      <span class="salute">Dear <span class="persName">George</span>—</span>


      <p>
         <span class="persName">Alger</span> was here when your wire came. You will meet Alger in Omaha. He has my route in full. get it. Say George. <span class="persName">Mead</span> was not paid neither was <span class="persName">Van Dreveldt</span>. And I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="do not​ ">donot</a>
    ​ know if any body was. <u>do</u>  
         <u>you</u>? I have sent for <span class="persName">Hymer</span> to meet you gentlemen in Omaha. Make him show up receipts &amp; step down and out of handling any money. let him work on commission Let <span class="persName">Heckert</span> take full charge of construction of <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="moving​ ">mooveing</a>
    ​ dirt if you think best. Lets get to work no more non sense— I will arrange if possible to help you all I can— as soon as I can I will pay Meade at once—</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">
            <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="Hastily​ ">Hastly</a>
    ​</span> 
         <br/>Bill

Held to showcase the world's cultural, agricultural, and manufacturing products and to promote civil liberties for women and African Americans, it would later become known as "the Atlanta Exposition." On September 18, Booker T. Washington gave a famous speech on race relations at the exhibition.
