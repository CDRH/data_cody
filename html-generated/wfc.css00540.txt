Oct. 24th 1900</span>


      <span class="salute">My Dear <span class="persName">Beck</span>—</span>


      <p>Have just wired you to immediately have coal <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="filing​ ">fileing</a>
    ​ <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="transfered​ ">trasfered</a>
    ​ to us— we should have learnt a lesson by this time— So that no one else can beat us— And not trust to our own opinion too much— I should have filed on that coal land my self— But you thought our segregation would hold it— So <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="let's​ ">lets</a>
    ​ take no more chances— Van Orsdel<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a> &amp; Burdick<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a> wanted to know as soon as we fixed <span class="persName">Darrahs</span> contest on land— so I wired them <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="also​ ">allso</a>
    ​— Morrill<a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a>—</p>

      <p>Get our matters safe quick or some one else will protest or jump coal land— <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="don't​ ">dont</a>
    ​ delay an hour in protecting our rights—</p>


      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Yours truly</span>


         <br/>W. F. Cody</span>


   </div>




   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Lafayette, Louisiana, on October 24, 1900.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: Josiah Alexander Van Orsdel (1860-1937) became a county prosecuting attorney of Laramie County, Wyoming, in 1892; a member of the Wyoming House of Representatives in 1894; the state attorney general of Wyoming from 1898 to 1905; an Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of Wyoming 1905-06; assistant to the U.S. Attorney General 1906-07; and U.S. federal judge from 1907 to 1937. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: Charles W. Burdick (1860-1927) was a prominent Cheyenne lawyer, and served as the first Wyoming state auditor (1890-94) and later as Wyoming secretary of state (1895-99).</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: Charles Henry Morrill (1843-1928) a prominent American businessman who was once president of the Lincoln Land Company. He would help the Burlington Railroad to find the best railroads through the Midwest territories. Cody was once a guide for Morrill exploring the Yellowstone Valley in Wyoming for the railroad. Morrill received invitations for the yearly hunting expeditions organized by Cody.</div>
