      <div class="note">Buffalo Bill's Wild West</div>


      <span class="dateline">Metz Germany<a href="../doc/n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
         <br/>


Sep. 1st 1906</span>


      <span class="salute">Dear <span class="persName">George</span>.</span>


      <p>If nothing happens to prevent I will leave Wapiti<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a> Oct 15th for a ten days hunt. I propose going up Elk fork get up on the Wapiti Ridge<a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a> and hunting the heads of Hard Pan<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a>. Bob Cat on the south heds of Fish hawk<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a>. Eagle<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a> and up wapiti ridge to head of Ishawooa<a href="../doc/n05" class="internal_link">5</a> &amp; down to TE<a href="../doc/n06" class="internal_link">6</a>. I will have but three or four particular friends with me. And I want you to be one of them I will send a Pack outfit to meet us at Wapiti starting from there Oct 15<u>th</u> I hope to arrive in Cody Oct 10<u>th</u>
      </p>


      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Your old friend</span>

         <br/>W. F. Cody</span>


      <p>PS. I hope the Citizens of Cody are urgeing the building of the high line Canal which I have written about before</p>

      <p>Germany is doing fine about $50000 a week-</p>


      <p>Bill</p>

   </div>





   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Metz, Germany, September 1-2, 1906.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: Wapiti is located twenty miles west of Cody, Wyoming, on the North Fork of the Shoshone River.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: Elk Fork, Hard Pan, Bobcat, Fish Hawk, and Eagle are tributary creeks that feed the north and south forks of the Shoshone River west of Cody, Wyoming.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: Wapiti Ridge lies between the north and south forks of the Shoshone River, west of Cody, Wyoming.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 5: Ishawooa Creek is located on the South Fork of the Shoshone River.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 6: TE Ranch, located on the South Fork of the Shoshone River, was owned by William F. Cody.</div>
