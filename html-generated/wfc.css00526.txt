      <div class="note">Buffalo Bill's Wild West</div>


      <span class="dateline">Chicago<a href="../doc/n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
         <br/>

July 28<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a>
      </span>

      <span class="salute">My Dear <span class="persName">Beck</span>
      </span>

      <p>Those Preists<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a> want their deeds to their Lots— I wish you would let me know if you are now ready and will make them out for them?</p>

      <p>Snyder<a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a> writes me you have gone South to work for <span class="persName">Alger</span>— not but what I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="approve​ ">approove</a>
    ​ of it. But I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="can't​ ">cant</a>
    ​ <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="believe​ ">beleive</a>
    ​ that you would leave until the water was again <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="running​ ">runing</a>
    ​. If you have left our business just at this critical time— Its not right And I will so notify the Co. I hope <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="it's​ ">its</a>
    ​ not true—</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Very truly yours</span>

         <br/>W. F. Cody</span>


   </div>



   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Chicago, Illinois, on July 25-30, 1898. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: The year is not inscribed but is 1898.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: "Priests" is reference to the Chicago clergy who promoted land sales and influenced their Polish and Bavarian parishioners to homestead in the Big Horn Basin.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: "Snyder" is William "Okie" Snyder (b.1856), William F. Cody's ranch manager who had taken 160 acres near Irma Flats. </div>
