      <div class="note">Buffalo Bill's Wild West </div>


      <span class="dateline">New Haven- Conn<a href="../doc/n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
         <br/>


June 28th 99</span>


      <span class="salute">My Dear <span class="persName">Beck</span>
      </span>


      <p>Your friendly letter recd— glad to get it. I am looking hourly for a <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="telegram​ ">telegrame</a>
    ​ saying not only our bridge<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a> has gone but the Corbett<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a>. if the bridges go out the pleasure trip for my family &amp; friends will be off. But if not they will be there or at Red Lodge<a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a> Aug 2d— And I thank you very much for your willingness to show them attention. Snyder<a href="../doc/n05" class="internal_link">5</a> was on and I told him what to do. But if he fails to do it— or if he insists on getting drunk— I wish you would take charge. But I have begged of Okey<a href="../doc/n05" class="internal_link">5</a> to do what is right. But <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="won't​ ">wont</a>
    ​ stand any more nonsense from him. By the enclosed <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="telegram​ ">telegrame</a>
    ​ you can see how our

Let Clark<a href="../doc/n06" class="internal_link">6</a> step in and for close then I will have to pay the $30,000 with interest. Then we will see who owns the layout—</p>

      <p>Am I not right?</p>

      <p>Mrs Cody<a href="../doc/n07" class="internal_link">7</a> invites Mrs Beck &amp; the wonderful Miss Beck<a href="../doc/n08" class="internal_link">8</a> to join her party for the Mountains<a href="../doc/n09" class="internal_link">9</a> when they get to Cody—</p>

      <p>How many tents can you scare up?</p>


      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Yours</span>


         <br/>Cody</span>

   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in New Haven, Connecticut, on June 28, 1899. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: "Bridge" over the Shoshone River near Cody; this may refer to the bridge in Corbett.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: Corbett Bridge, over the Shoshone River, lies a few miles northeast of Cody; Corbett was named after John Corbett who had a roadhouse at this location. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: Red Lodge, Montana, lies 65 miles north of Cody and was the closest rail line to Cody, Wyoming.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 5: "Snyder" and "Okey" refer to William "Okie" Snyder, one of Cody's men and a cowboy in Buffalo Bill's Wild West during 1896. Snyder had 160 acres near Irma Flats.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 6: Edward H. Clark (1864-1945) is the financial manager for Phoebe Hearst. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 7: Louisa Frederici Cody, Cody's wife. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 8: Daisy May Sorrenson Beck (Mrs. George Beck) and infant daughter Betty, born in 1898.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 9: George Beck and Mrs. Beck were to take Cody's family and the Frederic Remingtons to Carter Mountain Ranch and Irma Lake. Remington's work "Irma Going to Irma" pen and ink drawing of Cody's daughter Irma, is in the collection of the Buffalo Bill Center of the West.</div>
