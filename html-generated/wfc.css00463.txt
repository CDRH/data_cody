      <span class="salute">Dear <span class="persName">Geo</span>
      </span>

      <p>I close here Saturday Nov 2d<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a>— <u>Busted</u>. This is the worst place we have struck no one coming to this Fair. You must close down fast or if I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="can't​ ">cant</a>
    ​ get new blood<a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a> in we will be in an awful tight place— Do you appreciate what I mea[n] I will do my part but get down as easy on me as you possibly can this I have written you several times.</p>

      <p>I will give you my route as near as I can.  <del>wire</del> Nov. 6 &amp; 7. Continental Hotel Philadelphia Pa— <span class="persName">Salsburys</span> Address till Nov 16th Grand Opera House Philadelphia Pa Better direct there. I will try and be with <span class="persName">Geo Bleistein</span>. Buffalo. Oct 9th then we start for you<a href="../doc/n05" class="internal_link">5</a>—</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <br/>Bill
</span>
   </div>



   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Atlanta, Georgia, from October 28 - November 2, 1895, during the run of The Cotton States and International Exposition, also known as the Atlanta Exposition. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: The year is not inscribed by Cody but is 1895 as determined by the Routes List. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: Buffalo Bill's Wild West closed the season on November 2nd. The run in Atlanta had been very unprofitable.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: Cody writes that construction on the canal will need to be shut down if new investors are not found to keep the project going.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 5: A hunt in northwest Wyoming, for which Beck was preparing, was planned following the close of the season.</div>
