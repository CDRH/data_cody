Oct. 19th<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a>
      </span>

      <span class="salute">My Dear <span class="persName">Beck</span>
      </span>

      <p>No letter for several days Recd a letter from Arta<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a> Said she met <span class="persName">Mr Heckert</span> on train going to Sheridan after <u>Blankets</u>. I do hope you and he both did not leave the work at the same time. And its strange if Heckert had to go after <u>Blankets</u>. These things should have been thought if not why does so important a man have to go after blankets?</p>

      <p>I am uneasy about these things and over anxious as I am getting other peoples money to spend in this <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="enterprise​ ">enterprize</a>
    ​. And I want nothing done wrong. You are the Boss there. And My Dear Friend I hope you will take the same interest. I will feel awful if money is uselessly squandered there. And no good results to show. I may be there by

November 15th or 20<u>th</u> with the party. And I want them to feel that we are working on business principles. And doing our level best.</p>

      <p>Say. George.</p>

      <p>Please send me some more of that building rock Gypsom. Sulphur. Asphalt. And anything I can show or talk on. Send it quick. And please write me <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="what's​ ">whats</a>
    ​ being done and how you are doing it. And that you see that every body does an honest days work And no time is lost.</p>

      <p>If I get out there and these businessmen sees that the work is being extravagantly done— I fear they <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="won't​ ">wont</a>
    ​ put up any more money. For Gods sake hoof them up. </p>

      <span class="closer">
         <br/>Cody
</span>

      <p>PS. Denbeigh<a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a> has written me for his money.</p>

   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Macon, Georgia, on October 19, 1895.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: The year is not inscribed by Cody but is 1895 as determined by the Routes List.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: Arta Lucille Cody (Dec 16, 1866-Jan 30, 1904) was the eldest daughter of Louisa and William F. Cody. In the Cody family Bible, Cody recorded his daughter's name and birth as "Arta Lilly Elvira Cody. Born. Dec. 16th 1866. Leavenworth Kansas." </div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: "Denbeigh" may have been Frank Denbeigh (1865-1928), a miner working in Wyoming during 1895.</div>
