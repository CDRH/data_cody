      <p>PS. George. I wrote you to Sheridan that if I had the ready cash I would send you 3000 at once—I am putting every dollar I make with this show into Black America<a href="../doc/n09" class="internal_link">9</a> As <span class="persName">Salsbury</span> is still dangerously sick. <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="can't​ ">cant</a>

   <div class="note">Note 1: Letterhead is imprinted with "1895;" Cody does not inscribe the date, but Routes List and letter context determine the year as 1896.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: The Wyoming government body overseeing the Carey Act was the State Land Board in Cheyenne.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: "Water bond" likely refers to Cody seeking to secure water rights from the State of Wyoming to sell to the German migrant families who intended to settle in the Big Horn Basin.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: "President" is likely President Grover Cleveland, whom Cody was hoping would soon sign over the lands for reclamation and settlement in the Big Horn Basin under the Carey Act of 1894.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 5: Bronson Rumsey's brother was Laurence Dana Rumsey, Sr., 1849-1917.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 6: Red Bluffs is a hill formation to the south and west of Cody, Wyoming,  whose red color stands out among the mountainous formations.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 7: "Sulphur creek" is a natural streambed located west of Cody, Wyoming, flowing north toward the Shoshone River along the base of Cedar Mountain.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 8: The schedule Cody lists is his itinerary for late March to April 25: Buffalo Bill's Wild West opened the 1896 season in Philadelphia April 18-25, 1896.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 9: "Black America" was the traveling show staged by Nathan Salsbury in 1895 as a representation of Southern life; the troupe numbered 300 people and was the largest African American show ever put on a stage. </div>
