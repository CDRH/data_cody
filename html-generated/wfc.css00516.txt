      <span class="dateline">April 19-1897—<a href="../doc/n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
      </span>

      <p>The monied men of our Co. want to know what you are doing &amp; if you are going to get water to the town<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a> &amp; below. They say they are tired of such management and if you can't do the work they want to know it and at once.</p>

      <p>
         <span class="persName">George</span> you told me you would be at Cody to commence work by the 1<u>st</u> of April. God only know where you are and if you will ever get to Cody and if you will get to work when you do get there. If water <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="ain't​ ">aint</a>
    ​ down to town early we are ruined.</p> 

      <p>George if you don't try to do something this dam ditch will drive me crazy. Why won't you for once get down to work &amp; see that the men and or you work.</p>

      <p>For Gods sake do something. I can't stand the company off with fairy tales any longer—</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Yours</span>

         <br/>Cody</span>

   </div>



   <div class="note">Note 1: Letter is inscribed on lined notepaper by someone other than Cody, perhaps dictated by Cody to an employee. No salutation is inscribed, but it is written to George Beck.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: Cody, Wyoming.</div>
