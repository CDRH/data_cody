      <span class="salute">My. Dear. <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/beck.g">George</a>
         </span>—</span>

      <p>I sent <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/alger.h">Alger</a>
         </span> $3000— A letter from him. Saying wants 5 to 6000 more for 10th pay day. I will have to do it all as I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="believe​ &#xA;    ">beleive</a> others have got discouraged— as no water even to Sulphur creek yet— and can get no estimate on probable cost to finish up 25000 acre tract I admit I am getting discouraged my self— I have given up all hopes of your <u>ever</u> getting to Sulphur Creek<a name="n02.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n02.note" class="ref">2</a>— or getting an estimate on cost to finish— 25000 acres— Never heard if <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/mead.e">Mead</a>
         </span> went up with you. I thought if he went up there he would give us an estimate on something—</p>

      <p>I will have to do the putting up— and I know nothing of your expenses or what you are doing.</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <br/>Cody</span>

         <a name="n01.note"> </a>Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Cincinnati, Ohio, on May 4-5, 1896. The day's entry in the 1896 Route Diary: "Cincinnati, Ohio. / Tuesday, May 5. Weather fine. J. Frost, of the Irish Lancers, was taken sick with pneumonia and went to hospital to-day. Gay Hudson Piller, Cowboy rider, went over the ropes to-night; the horse knocked down a beacon light, but no one was hurt. Tambourine McCarty, Ken a Kamis and wife, the Japanese fencers, of the Annex, closed here. J. T. McCaddon and M. B. Bailey left for Chicago on the 8.30 train on business for the company. / Business in the afternoon big, in the evening immense. / Lot, Ludlow avenue and Milk Creek, Cumminsville. / Arena, 169 x 430."<a href="#n01.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n02.note"> </a>Note 2: "Sulphur Creek" is a natural streambed located west of Cody, Wyoming, flowing north toward the Shoshone River along the base of Cedar Mountain.<a href="#n02.ref"> [back]</a>
