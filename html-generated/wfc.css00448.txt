      <span class="dateline">Cortland, N. Y. Sep. 15 1895<a name="n01.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n01.note" class="ref">1</a>
      </span>

      <span class="salute">Dear <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/beck.g">George</a>
         </span>.</span>

      <p>I expected a letters here to day from You and Heck. Telling me what you had done. Did you get a secretary? If so let him write me I know too well what poor letter writers you and <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/heckart.t">Heck</a>
         </span> are. I sent Curley<a name="n02.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n02.note" class="ref">2</a> to <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/bleistein.g">George Bleistein</a>
         </span> from Elmira Say George, I know you <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="don't​ &#xA;    ">dont</a> like <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/hymer.w">Hymer</a>
         </span>— But he is a pusher. And thats the kind of men we want with this big job. And one can


<span class="hr"> </span>
         <span class="pageimage">
            <a href="http://codyarchive.org/figures/800/wfc.css00448.002.jpg"
               rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]"

               <img src="http://codyarchive.org/figures/250/wfc.css00448.002.jpg"
                    class="display &#xA;            "/>
            </a>
         </span>

catch more flies with molasses than with vinegar So pat him on the back— Say did you send the <u>stock</u> for Bleistein. <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/salsbury.n">Salsbury</a>
         </span> &amp; Myself? Do you know what Heck done with the Transportation Co<a name="n03.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n03.note" class="ref">3</a> at Sheridan. I got a wire from Paxton &amp; Gallagher <a name="n04.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n04.note" class="ref">4</a>&amp; from Smith &amp; Co—<a name="n05.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n05.note" class="ref">5</a> of Omaha. Asking me to guarantee Heckarts orders for supplies and furnishing goods which I did. I wish some one would keep me posted on matters at that end. So I would know what is being done— And then there will be no mistake and I can understand and help to protect our credit

You did not write me as you promised and explain all about the co— That point that I wanted to know about being a Stock Co— And if any of the old co— can give us any trouble. And if the State Land Board is going to give us a contract for our land &amp; if you settled with Ryan and his partner about their water right. You know we broke our contract with


<span class="hr"> </span>
         <span class="pageimage">
            <a href="http://codyarchive.org/figures/800/wfc.css00448.004.jpg"
               rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]"
               title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://codyarchive.org/figures/800/wfc.css00448.004.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">

them by not paying them when we agreed to— and they can make us dance to their music if they choose to. I know old man I am over nervous about these things. But we have got our friends into this. And we must protect them. Our everlasting reputation is at stake. All our energy &amp; what brains we posess must be brought into actions Nothing must be left undone— George— I look to you to attend to this until I can get there to help you—</p>

      <p>Write me often. Do keep me posted— Tell Heck to write me—</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Your friend &amp; pard</span>

         <br/>W. F. Cody
</span>

      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n04.note"> </a>Note 4: The Paxton-Gallagher business became renowned for its wholesale groceries, including Butternut coffee. The Paxton &amp; Gallagher Wholesale Grocery Company was founded in 1879 by William Paxton and Benjamin Gallagher. Gallagher was a grocery man, and operated a series of companies all along the Union Pacific railroad route.<a href="#n04.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n05.note"> </a>Note 5: M. E. Smith &amp; Co. was a wholesale dry goods business located in Omaha, Nebraska. The company began in 1868 and was acquired by the Bryne-Hammer Co. in 1925 for $1,830,000.<a href="#n05.ref"> [back]</a>
