      <span class="salute">Dear <span class="persName">George</span>.</span>

      <p>I expected a letters here to day from You and Heck. Telling me what you had done. Did you get a secretary? If so let him write me I know too well what poor letter writers you and <span class="persName">Heck</span> are. I sent Curley<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a> to <span class="persName">George Bleistein</span> from Elmira Say George, I know you <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="don't​ ">dont</a>
    ​ like <span class="persName">Hymer</span>— But he is a pusher. And thats the kind of men we want with this big job. And one can

Do you know what Heck done with the Transportation Co<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a> at Sheridan. I got a wire from Paxton &amp; Gallagher <a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a>&amp; from Smith &amp; Co—<a href="../doc/n05" class="internal_link">5</a> of Omaha. Asking me to guarantee Heckarts orders for supplies and furnishing goods which I did. I wish some one would keep me posted on matters at that end. So I would know what is being done— And then there will be no mistake and I can understand and help to protect our credit

You did not write me as you promised and explain all about the co— That point that I wanted to know about being a Stock Co— And if any of the old co— can give us any trouble. And if the State Land Board is going to give us a contract for our land &amp; if you settled with Ryan and his partner about their water right. You know we broke our contract with

them by not paying them when we agreed to— and they can make us dance to their music if they choose to. I know old man I am over nervous about these things. But we have got our friends into this. And we must protect them. Our everlasting reputation is at stake. All our energy &amp; what brains we posess must be brought into actions Nothing must be left undone— George— I look to you to attend to this until I can get there to help you—</p>

      <p>Write me often. Do keep me posted— Tell Heck to write me—</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Your friend &amp; pard</span>

         <br/>W. F. Cody

   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Ithaca, New York, on September 14, 1895. There was no Sunday performance on September 15th.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: "Curley" is unidentified.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: The transportation company in Sheridan was owned by Cody and known as the W. F. Cody Transportation Company.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: The Paxton-Gallagher business became renowned for its wholesale groceries, including Butternut coffee. The Paxton &amp; Gallagher Wholesale Grocery Company was founded in 1879 by William Paxton and Benjamin Gallagher. Gallagher was a grocery man, and operated a series of companies all along the Union Pacific railroad route.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 5: M. E. Smith &amp; Co. was a wholesale dry goods business located in Omaha, Nebraska. The company began in 1868 and was acquired by the Bryne-Hammer Co. in 1925 for $1,830,000.</div>
