      <div class="note">Buffalo Bill's Wild West</div>



      <span class="dateline">Gladback<a href="../doc/n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
         <br/>

Sep. 8th / 06</span>


      <span class="salute">Dear <span class="persName">George</span>—</span>

 
      <p>Your letter came to day And as Geo. B. Harris<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a> is so anxious to know what the people of Cody &amp; my self are doing about the high line canal— I took the liberty of sending him your letter.</p>

      <p>Well George— <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="Yesterday​ ">Yestarday</a>
    ​ Mr Geo. O. Starr<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a> Presedent of the Barnum &amp; Bailey Ltd. Co. came over from America to see me— About showing in America. And I signed the best show contract for Little Willie<a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a> that he has ever had— And the first good one I have ever had. Mr Starr left soon as the contract was signed for America to commence building an entire new outfit for me— 60 new cars— &amp;c. I got L<a href="../doc/n05" class="internal_link">5</a> in Hungary &amp; Poland but have <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="pulled​ ">puled</a>
    ​ out in Germany.</p>

      <p>
         <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="I'll​ ">Ill</a>
    ​ tell you all about it in the Rockies when we make our ten days hunt</p>


      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Love to family. Kiss Betty<a href="../doc/n06" class="internal_link">6</a> for me</span>


         <br/>Col</span>

   </div>




   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed at Monchengladbach, Germany, on September 8, 1906. Cody abbreviates the German city as "Gladback." </div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: George B. Harris (1866-1918), second vice-president of the C. B. &amp; Q. Railroad, became president of the company in 1901 until his resignation in 1910.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: George O. Starr (1849-1915) began his show career in a dime museum in the Bowery, becoming the press agent for P. T. Barnum's circus in 1878; by 1905 he was general manager for Barnum &amp; Bailey.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: "Little Willie" is Cody's reference to himself.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 5: Hell.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 6: "Betty" refers to George Beck's daughter who was born in Cody, Wyoming, in 1898.</div>
