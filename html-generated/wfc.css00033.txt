      <div class="note">Buffalo Bill's Wild West Combined With Pawnee Bill's Great Far East.</div>

      <span class="dateline">
Zanesville, O.
Jun. 29th<a href="../doc/n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
      </span>

      <span class="salute">The Elwells<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a>
         <br/>
My Very Dear Friends</span>



      <p>So glad to get your letters and know that you are well and prosperous. But still the Call of the Wild is in your hearts. How I long for the Mountains and sage brush. I cannot be truly happy away from there. <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="Maybe​ ">Maby</a>
    ​ we may meet out in our glorious Mountains again. I am well.</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Love to all</span>
         <br/>Col
</span>

      <p>My grand son Cody<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a> is at Pahaska<a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a>. and the other day killed a Bear— The help had sit up with him 3 nights</p>

   </div>






   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West combined with Pawnee Bill's Great Far East performed in Zanesville, Ohio, on June 29, 1912.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: <span class="persName">Robert Farrington Elwell</span> (1874-1962) and his wife, Mary Ellen (Taylor) Elwell.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: Most likely William Cody Boal (1896-1962), son of Arta Cody.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: Pahaska Tepee, a hunting and tourist lodge opened by William F. Cody near the east entrance to Yellowstone National Park in 1904.</div>
