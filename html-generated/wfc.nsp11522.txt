      <h3>WHAT IS GOING ON IN THE THEATRICAL WORLD.</h3>

      <div class="milestone line"> </div>

      <div class="inline_figure">
         <div class="p">[illustration]</div>
      </div>

      <p>THE Red Star ss. Switzerland, which arrived at Antwerp on Wednesday from Philadelphia, brought <span class="persName">Major John M. Burke</span>, seventy Indians, and twenty white people of the Buffalo Bill Wild West Company.</p>

      <p>Among the palefaces are "<span class="persName">Johnnie Baker</span>," Miss Della Farrell, Mr. and Mrs. J. W. Comrie, Mr. and Mrs. <span class="persName">George C. Crager</span> and <span class="persName">John Nelson</span>. Of the Indians in the list of passengers of the Switzerland, the following are some of the more characteristic and poetic names:&#x97; Comes-Out-Holy, Two-Bonnet, Brings-Yellow-Horses, Fog, Holy Bird, Both-Sides-White, Pull-Him-Out, Her Blanket, Plenty-Blankets, Wounded-With-Many-Arrows, Run-Along-Side-Off, Close-to-Home, Hard-to-Hit, Mrs. Crow Cane, Know-His-Voice, Coming Grunt, Bring-the-White, Mrs. Call-the-Name.</p>

      <p>On reaching Antwerp the party took a special train for Strasburg, near which place the rest of the Wild West Company have been wintering.</p>
