Oct— 3d<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a>

      <span class="salute">Dear <span class="persName">George</span>—</span>

      <p>Here is some good news.  R. Law<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a> is the supt of Burlington R.R. in Iowa. Mr Law is the supt who has opened up the coal fields and built roads to them for the Burlington in Missouri &amp; Iowa<a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a>— And is <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="really​ ">realy</a>
    ​ the coal expert for the Burlington and the practiced rail road builder. Mr Law is coming out with me to look the coal fields<a href="../doc/n05" class="internal_link">5</a> over. And the most practical route to the Park<a href="../doc/n06" class="internal_link">6</a> from what I can learn they will soon push through our country. If they do you know what that means to our country. We leave Omaha Nov 6<u>th</u>
      </p>

      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Write me often</span> 

         <br/>Col</span>


   </div>



   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Oskaloosa, Iowa, on October 3, 1896. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: The year is not inscribed by Cody but is 1896.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: "R. Law" is Robert R. Law (1847-unknown) who served as superintendent for several associated railroad systems in the 1870s and 1880s, including the Chicago, Burlington, &amp; Kansas City; the Burlington &amp; Western; the Burlington &amp; Northwestern; as well as the Union Pacific in Nebraska and the Montana Union of Butte and Anaconda. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: Burlington and Missouri River Railroad Company.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 5: Most likely the coal fields were the Bearcreek Mines, located at Bearcreek, Montana, about eight miles southeast of Red Lodge, Montana.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 6: Yellowstone National Park.</div>
