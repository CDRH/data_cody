      <h6 class="fw">The Sporting Chronicle.</h6>
      <h6 class="fw">THURSDAY, MAY 3, 1888.</h6>

      <p>SALE OF BUFFALO BILL'S STOCK.—Yesterday forenoon a sale of the surplus stock of animals belonging to the "Wild West" Show took place at the Manchester Racecourse, when there was a good attendance of buyers and spectators. Mr. Cross,<a name="n01.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n01.note" class="ref">1</a> the well-known Liverpool naturalist, was present and secured the principal wild animals. A large buffalo was knocked down to him for £101, after some spirited bidding; and he also secured three elks for £19, £27, and £26 respectively. The well-known circus proprietors, Messrs. Sanger,<a name="n02.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n02.note" class="ref">2</a> were also represented, and to them went a pair of grey mules for £31, a she elk for £21, two American deer for £10, a pair of reindeer, with sledge and harness, for £40; besides several horses. A pair of splendid carriage horses were bought by Mr. Goodall,<a name="n03.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n03.note" class="ref">3</a> of Altrincham, for £80; and a bear fell to Mr. Cross for £20. For the remainder of the horses and ponies the prices reached from £5 10s. to £24 10s.</p>
