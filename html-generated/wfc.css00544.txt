      <div class="note">Buffalo Bill's Wild West</div>



      <span class="dateline">Brooklyn<a href="#n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
         <br/>

May 9th<a href="#n02" class="internal_link">2</a>
      </span>

      <span class="salute">My Dear <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/beck.g">George</a>
         </span>
      </span>


      <p>Have you sent the Bond back to Mr Clark<a href="#n03" class="internal_link">3</a> 
         <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="it's​ &#xA;    ">its</a> necessary he has it soon as possible. Say that contract with Wallenburg<a href="#n04" class="internal_link">4</a>— does it state when Wallenburg must pay for land? May be we can get him on that clause— Let me know what Sisters<a href="#n05" class="internal_link">5</a> first payment on her 160 is &amp; I will send the money to you</p>

      <p>Business is great with me.</p>

      <p>When did you get water in town do you think you can keep it coming?</p>


      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Your friend</span>  


         <br/>W F Cody</span>

   </div>

      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n04.note"/>Note 4: "Wallenburg" is likely Gustav Wallenburg, a Swedish immigrant from Chicago, who worked as a land agent in the Big Horn Basin; in 1907 Wallenburg filed but lost a lawsuit against Shoshone Irrigation Company.<a href="#n04.ref">  [back]
            </a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n05.note"/>Note 5: "Sisters" is a reference to the payment owed by Helen Cody Wetmore (1850-1911), William F. Cody's sister.<a href="#n05.ref">  [back]
