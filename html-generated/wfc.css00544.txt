      <p>Have you sent the Bond back to Mr Clark<a name="n03.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n03.note" class="ref">3</a> 
         <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="it's​ &#xA;    ">its</a> necessary he has it soon as possible. Say that contract with Wallenburg<a name="n04.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n04.note" class="ref">4</a>— does it state when Wallenburg must pay for land? May be we can get him on that clause— Let me know what Sisters<a name="n05.ref"> </a>
         <a href="#n05.note" class="ref">5</a> first payment on her 160 is &amp; I will send the money to you</p>

      <p>Business is great with me.</p>

      <p>When did you get water in town do you think you can keep it coming?</p>


      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Your friend</span>  


         <br/>W F Cody</span>

   <div class="bibliography">
      <p>
         <a name="n01.note"> </a>Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Brooklyn, New York, May 5-10, 1902.<a href="#n01.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n02.note"> </a>Note 2: The year is not inscribed by Cody but is 1902.<a href="#n02.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n03.note"> </a>Note 3: "Mr Clark" may be E. H. Clark, financial manager for Phoebe Hearst, from whom Beck had secured a loan for Shoshone Irrigation Company.<a href="#n03.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n04.note"> </a>Note 4: "Wallenburg" is likely Gustav Wallenburg, a Swedish immigrant from Chicago, who worked as a land agent in the Big Horn Basin; in 1907 Wallenburg filed but lost a lawsuit against Shoshone Irrigation Company.<a href="#n04.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
      <p>
         <a name="n05.note"> </a>Note 5: "Sisters" is a reference to the payment owed by Helen Cody Wetmore (1850-1911), William F. Cody's sister.<a href="#n05.ref"> [back]</a>
      </p>
