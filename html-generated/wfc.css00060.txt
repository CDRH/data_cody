North Platte, Neb,

Apr. 26th 1886
</span>
      <span class="salute">My Dear <span class="persName">Mollie</span>—</span>


      <p>Enclosed find a P.O. for $25.<a href="../doc/n01" class="internal_link">1</a> I presume you are getting about ready to come to St. Louis. Wish you would start from home in time to arrive in St. Louis about the 2nd or <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="3rd​ ">3th</a>
    ​ of May. Go to the. St. James Hotel if I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="ain't​ ">aint</a>
    ​ there to meet you. I will be there any how by the <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="3rd​ ">3th</a>
    ​.<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a> I have got you the white horse. and a fine saddle. Suppose you have your habit.<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a>
      </p>

      <p>Will be glad to see you—</p>
      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">With Love.</span>

         <br/>W. F. C.
</span>
   </div>




   <div class="note">Note 1: A postal money order.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: Buffalo Bill's Wild West opened its 1886 season in St. Louis on May 10.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: Cody refers to a "riding habit," a set of tailored clothing then considered proper dress for women riding horseback.</div>
