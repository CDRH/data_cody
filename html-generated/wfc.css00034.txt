      <div class="note">Buffalo Bill's Wild West</div>
      <span class="dateline">

Pittsburg

May 29<a href="../doc/n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
      </span>

      <span class="salute">My. Dear Elwell<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a>
      </span>



      <p>Glad to get your nice letter—From camp—Its strange to me—that people cant learn to <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="always​ ">allways</a>
    ​ set there camps in <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="secluded​ ">se cluded</a>
    ​ places out of the wind. This should be done— Speak to Mr Kelsey. <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="also​ ">allso</a>
    ​ try and locate the camp where there is grass—I am surprised that active work was not commenced at once—Tell Mr Martin<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a> to let you keep the Pony for your use—Will be glad to see your sketches &amp; letter in the Enterprise<a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a>. I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="haven't​ ">havent </a>
    ​ heard from Mrs Elwell<a href="../doc/n05" class="internal_link">5</a>—if she got the money—Keep the Mexican saddle for your use <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="Don't​ ">Dont</a>
    ​ bother about going to TE<a href="../doc/n06" class="internal_link">6</a>— to measure the land—till you are there some time. Do you think water can be taken out of the river by a head gate and put on the truck—</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <br/>Cody
</span>
   </div>





   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Pittsburgh on 28 and 29 May 1901.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: <span class="persName"/>Robert Farrington Elwell (1874-1962)</div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: Probably John Martin, who was then an agent for William F. Cody's business interests in the town that bore his name.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 5: The <em class="hi">Cody Enterprise</em>, a newspaper founded by William F. Cody in 1899, still the main local newspaper in the town of Cody, Wyoming.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 6: R.F. Elwell had married Mary Ellen Taylor (born c. 1875) in 1897.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 7: The TE Ranch, located on the South Fork of the Shoshone River, was a favorite retreat for Cody in the last two decades of his life.</div>
