Oct—7<u>th</u> 96</span>

      <span class="salute">Dear <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/beck.g">George</a>
         </span>—</span>

      <p>Yours recd.  Well every man in the Co. have throwed up there tail but good old staying <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/salsbury.n">Salsbury</a>
         </span>. I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="can't​ &#xA;    ">cant</a> hear a word from <span class="persName">
            <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/cody_archive/doc/alger.h">Alger</a>
         </span>— only when he wires for money. I have <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="begged​ &#xA;    ">beged</a> him to let me know who the Co. owe so I could give them my personal note. No one will let me know what the pay roll will be up to Nov 3d. I think Alger is playing me a dirty stinking trick— When I am the only Man he has to depend on for money. You see I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="don't​ &#xA;    ">dont</a> know who he has paid &amp; how much the Co owe. He <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="won't​ &#xA;    ">wont</a> even <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="acknowledge​ &#xA;    ">acknowlige</a> receipt of money I sent him. I tell you bankruptcy stares us in the face— Salsbury says

         <a href="#n02.note" class="ref">[2]</a>, But I am glad you had nerve enough to keep the force at work— <u>Now</u> if you want them paid you must let me know how much it will be up to Nov 3<u>d</u>— and how near you will be to finishing to Town<a name="n03.ref"> </a>

      <p>And Alger can go to hell— If you will let me know— If some one <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="won't​ &#xA;    ">wont</a> tell me. I will not try to rain the money. <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="that's​ &#xA;    ">thats</a> all there is about it—</p>
