Oct—7<u>th</u> 96</span>

      <span class="salute">Dear <span class="persName">George</span>—</span>

      <p>Yours recd.  Well every man in the Co. have throwed up there tail but good old staying <span class="persName">Salsbury</span>. I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="can't​ ">cant</a>
    ​ hear a word from <span class="persName">Alger</span>— only when he wires for money. I have <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="begged​ ">beged</a>
    ​ him to let me know who the Co. owe so I could give them my personal note. No one will let me know what the pay roll will be up to Nov 3d. I think Alger is playing me a dirty stinking trick— When I am the only Man he has to depend on for money. You see I <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="don't​ ">dont</a>
    ​ know who he has paid &amp; how much the Co owe. He <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="won't​ ">wont</a>
    ​ even <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="acknowledge​ ">acknowlige</a>
    ​ receipt of money I sent him. I tell you bankruptcy stares us in the face— Salsbury says

he <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="won't​ ">wont</a>
    ​ carry the Buffalo parties<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a>, But I am glad you had nerve enough to keep the force at work— <u>Now</u> if you want them paid you must let me know how much it will be up to Nov 3<u>d</u>— and how near you will be to finishing to Town<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a> by that time. Will you do this? You can give me some idea of the amount of money it will take—</p>

      <p>And Alger can go to hell— If you will let me know— If some one <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="won't​ ">wont</a>
    ​ tell me. I will not try to rain the money. <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="that's​ ">thats</a>
    ​ all there is about it—</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <br/>Cody</span>
