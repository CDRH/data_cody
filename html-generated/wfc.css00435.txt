      <span class="dateline">Pottsville. Pa. May 5 1895<a href="../doc/n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
      </span>

      <span class="salute">My Dear <span class="persName">Beck</span>—</span>

      <p>Your wire <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="received​ ">recd.</a>
    ​ was surprised to know that you are still waiting on Paxton. Say old fellow are we going to <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="lose​ ">loose</a>
    ​ all this summer and get nothing done. If Mr Paxton had not told me he would go I would have made different arrangements— Now George will you write and let me know if the Paxton outfit is going to do anything. If not I would like to know it. We would never got our <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="concession​ ">consession</a>
    ​ if I had not pushed it.<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a> And now I am going on with it if I go it alone— So take a <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="moment's​ ">moments</a>
    ​ time &amp; write me— </p>

      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Awfully busy</span>

         <br/>Cody
</span>
   </div>



   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Pottsville, Pennsylvania, on May 6, 1895.  There was no performance on May 5, a Sunday.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: The concession to which Cody refers was a land segregation under the terms of the Carey Act.  The segregated land was intended to be irrigated by the Cody Canal.</div>
