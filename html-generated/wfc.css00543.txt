      <span class="dateline">May 4th 1902<a href="../doc/n01" class="internal_link">1</a>
      </span>


      <span class="salute">My Dear <span class="persName">Beck</span>—</span>

      <p>Yours received Am glad you can keep the water coming. What have you done with <span class="persName">Alger</span> 
         <span class="persName">Salsbury</span> says for you as Manager to demand a settlement. tell him to send in his bill for salary— &amp; you refer it to the company. if he <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="won't​ ">wont</a>
    ​ do it make him do it by law.</p>

      <p>
         <span class="persName">Bleistein</span> will remit I guess at once. Wrote Martin<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a> to pay my water rights assessment. Mrs Wetmore<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a> will send her payment on land

soon. Kelsey<a href="../doc/n04" class="internal_link">4</a> writes me he has sent maps &amp; report of survey— I presume you have let <span class="persName">Wiley</span>
         <a href="../doc/n05" class="internal_link">5</a> use them as agreed.</p>

      <p>The Wild West closed big engagement here open Brooklyn tomorrow sent route—</p>

      <p>The Gold Mine<a href="../doc/n06" class="internal_link">6</a> is a winner I guess beyond doubt its being kept quiet just now as we want some property Adjoining it and a water right from a stream near by. We are getting out ore and the vein <a rel="tooltip" class="sic" title="prove​ ">proove</a>
    ​ a true fissure</p>


      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Yours truly</span>


         <br/>W. F. Cody</span>

   </div>





   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in New York City, New York, from April 21 through May 3, 1902; no performance given on Sunday, May 4, 1902.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: John Henry Martin (d.1910), a Wyoming land agent who helped Cody. Martin was at one time manager of William F. Cody's interests in Cody, Wyoming.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: Helen Cody Wetmore (1850-1911), William F. Cody's sister. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 4: Frank C. Kelsey (c.1863-1933), a civil engineer. In 1901, William F. Cody hired Kelsey (who was then city engineer for Salt Lake City) to make a preliminary survey for the Cody-Salsbury Canal.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 5: Solon Lysander Wiley (1840-1926), a hydraulic engineer and contractor. </div>

   <div class="note">Note 6: "Gold Mine" may be a reference to Campo Bonito, a gold mine near Oracle, Arizona, leased by Cody-Dyer Mining and Milling Company.</div>
