      <span class="salute">My. Dear Mr <span class="persName">Woods</span>
      </span>


      <p>Mr Martin writes me there has been trouble at the hotel.<a href="../doc/n02" class="internal_link">2</a> Men quitting and fighting because the non arrival of Mr Harry Stevens—<a href="../doc/n03" class="internal_link">3</a> I have wired you twice without an answer. Whats the matter what am I to expect.</p>

      <p>Will you let me hear from you? Is Stevens going to finish his contract if so when?</p>

      <span class="closer">
         <span class="salute">Very truly yours</span>

         <br/>W. F. Cody

   <div class="note">Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Walla Walla, Washington, on August 21, 1902.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 2: Cody refers to his Irma Hotel, then under construction in the town of Cody, Wyoming. "Mr. Martin" is most likely John Henry Martin (d. 1910), a former Indianapolis railroad agent who was then Buffalo Bill's local representative and business manager.</div>

   <div class="note">Note 3: "Harry Stevens" was actually Henry A. Stephens, an owner of the Stephens Bros. construction firm, then located at 2418 N Street in Lincoln, Nebraska. On August 16, 1902, the  <em class="hi">Lincoln Trade Review</em> reported that Stephens Bros. was the general contractor for William F. Cody's Irma Hotel in Cody, Wyoming.</div>
