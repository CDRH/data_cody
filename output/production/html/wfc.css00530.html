Note 1: "Buffalo parties" refers to the investors from Buffalo, N.Y., primarily Bleistein , Gerrans , and Rumsey .
Note 2: "Bridge" refers to Cody's efforts to finance building a bridge over the Shoshone River near Cody.
Note 3: "Flats" refers to acreage in the South Fork valley south of Cody, Wyoming, that would later become known as "Irma Flats," named after William F. Cody's youngest daughter.
Note 4: Carbon County Bank was located in Red Lodge, Montana, the county seat; the bank was built before 1900.
