Buffalo Bill's Wild West
Brooklyn1
May 9th 2 My Dear George

Have you sent the Bond back to Mr Clark3 its necessary he has it soon as possible. Say that contract with Wallenburg4— does it state when Wallenburg must pay for land? May be we can get him on that clause— Let me know what Sisters5 first payment on her 160 is & I will send the money to you

Business is great with me.

When did you get water in town do you think you can keep it coming?

Your friend W F Cody
Note 1: Buffalo Bill's Wild West performed in Brooklyn, New York, May 5-10, 1902.
Note 2: The year is not inscribed by Cody but is 1902.
Note 3: "Mr Clark" may be E. H. Clark, financial manager for Phoebe Hearst, from whom Beck had secured a loan for Shoshone Irrigation Company.
Note 4: "Wallenburg" is likely Gustav Wallenburg, a Swedish immigrant from Chicago, who worked as a land agent in the Big Horn Basin; in 1907 Wallenburg filed but lost a lawsuit against Shoshone Irrigation Company.
Note 5: "Sisters" is a reference to the payment owed by Helen Cody Wetmore (1850-1911), William F. Cody's sister.
